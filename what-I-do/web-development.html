Web Development - The Backstory


In the early spring of 2021 I didn’t have the slightest idea of what HTML stood for… or CSS. Not only did I not not know the meaning of either, but I had also been furloughed from my admin position at a law firm due to the pandemic. Looking back now, I’ve realized that getting furloughed was exactly what I needed at that point in time. I was jobless and completely lost.


I reached out to my dad and asked him what he would do if he were in my position. He recommended I enroll in a full stack coding bootcamp. I wasn’t sure why this was his recommendation at the time, but I trusted him. I enrolled and was more anxious than I had ever been. Imposter syndrome had set in before I even touched my first line of code.


March of 2021 rolls around and I’m beginning my pre-work for UT Austin’s full stack coding bootcamp. I began writing some extremely basic HTML, and fell in love when I saw the changes I made locally appear in my browser. At that point I probably still didn’t know what HTML stood for, but I did know that you could do some seriously cool stuff with it. As I began learning CSS and JavaScript I realized the power and importance of even basic web development. I was hooked.
